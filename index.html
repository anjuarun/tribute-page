MOTHER TERESA
(August 26, 1910-September 5, 1997)







Mother Teresa was the founder of the Order of the Missionaries of Charity, a Roman Catholic congregation of women dedicated to helping the poor. Considered one of the greatest humanitarians of the 20th century, she was canonized as Saint Teresa of Calcutta in 2016.

  • Born: August 26, 1910, Skopje, Macedonia (FYROM) with name Agnes Gonxha Bojaxhiu
  • Agnes left home in September 1928, for the Loreto Convent in Rathfarnam (Dublin)
  • Agnes arrived in Calcutta on 6 January 1929
  • She made her final profession as a Loreto nun on 24 May 1937, and hereafter was called Mother Teresa
  • On December 21 1948, she went for the first time to the slums.
  • She visited families, washed the sores of some children, cared for an old man lying sick on the road and nursed a woman dying of hunger and tuberculosis
  • October 7, 1950, the new congregation of the Missionaries of Charity was officially erected
  • They run homes for people dying of HIV/AIDS, leprosy and tuberculosis; soup kitchens; dispensaries and mobile clinics; children's and family counselling programmes; orphanages; and schools.
  • In 1952, Mother Teresa opened the first Home for the Dying in space made available by the city of Calcutta.
  • Indian Padmashri Award in 1962
  • Nobel Peace Prize in 1979,
  • She continued to receive major Indian awards in subsequent years, including India's highest civilian award, the Bharat Ratna, in 1980
  • Died: September 5, 1997, Kolkata, India
  • She was canonised (recognised by the church as a saint) on 4 September 2016, and the anniversary of her death, 5 September, was made her feast day.
